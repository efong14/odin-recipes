Gyudon

Description

A simple and easy to cook beef rice bowl.

This recipe details how to use simple and inexpensive ingredients to make a fast and hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 green onions/scallions
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced beef (chuck or rib eye
  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp sake
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice, to serve
  • Pickled red ginger, to serve (optional)

Steps

  1. Thinly slice the onion.
  2. Next, cut the green onions diagonally into thin slices.
  3. Then, take out the meat from the freezer. Cut the sliced meat into pieces 3 inches (7.6 cm) wide.
  4. In a large frying pan (do not turn on the heat yet), add the dashi, sake, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar. Mix to combine.
  5. Next, add the onion slices and spread them throughout the pan, separating the onion layers.
  6. Then, add the meat on top of the onions. Separate the thin slices of beef so the meat covers the onions.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and start cooking over medium heat.
  8. When the meat is cooked, skim off the scum and fat from the broth with a fine-mesh skimmer. Turn down the heat to simmer and continue cooking, covered, for 3-4 minutes.
  9. Sprinkle the green onions on top and cook covered for another minute. If you like to add beaten eggs, you can add at this step
  10. In a large bowl, add steamed rice and drizzle the remaining sauce on top.
